General.

We are the National Society of Black Engineers, a club at Wheaton High School!

The mission of NSBE to increase the number of culturally responsible Black Engineers who excel academically, succeed professionally and positively impact the community. The WHS branch meets on Thursdays from 3:30pm to 5pm, and does a variety of activities and events, ranging from team competitions to fundraisers. Every year, we go to the NSBE National Convention (usually during March/April), where we meet other high school branches, as well as NSBE members from all walks of life. At the event, we participate in the VEX, TMAL, and Ten80 national competitions, which you can learn more about below!

VEX.

Build user-controlled robots to fufill an objective.

In VEX, we design, construct, and program a robot with VEX parts to fufill a yearly objective set by the VEX National Competition. The robot is programmed with VEXcode but is compatible with languages such as Java as well. We also go to regional competitions if our robot is finished before the event. VEX has additional meetings on Wendsday closer to the competition date. Last year, we were quarterfinalists. Here is a link to this year's competition!

Ten80.

Program an autonomous racecar to get the fastest time.

In Ten80, we assemble and program an autonomous racecar using an Arduino, in C. The car comes with various sensors you can use to navigate the track and detect obstacles. At the national competition, each racecar will complete a predetermined amount of laps as fast as they can. We have multiple cars to practice assembly and programming, as well as to run live tests on before the event.

TMAL.

Compete in a team-based math jeapordy game.

In TMAL (TriMAthaLon), we train to participate in a team based math competition, separated into Lower and Upper Division, for under and upperclassmen, respectively. Lower Division mainly covers geometry and algebra, while Upper Division includes some calculus as well. All of the material tested will be material already covered in your math classes. We participate in both Regional and National TMAL competitions. During club meetings, we run mock competitions to practice. Last year, we won first place regionally and fourth place nationally at the events!
